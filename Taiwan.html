Taiwan






The Most Famous Places



Yushan National Park

Yushan National Park (Chinese: 玉山國家公園) is one of the nine national parks in Taiwan and was named after the summit Yushan, the highest peak of the park.The park covers a total of 103,121 hectares including large sections of the Central Mountain Range. The park contains over thirty peaks more than 3,000 meters in elevation, and two-thirds of the area within the park is above 2,000 meters. The elevation difference in the park is 3,600 meters, and there are many canyons, cliffs, and valleys.

Kenting Street

This street becomes a lively market at night, when it is filled with food stalls & street vendors. Many visitors may find many stores and vendors selling souvenirs and accessories, food stalls and restaurants serving a wide array of traditional Taiwanese snacks such as braised food, grilled seafood, mochi, prawn crackers, as well as exotic cuisines ranging from European, Korean, Indian, Italian, to Thai. Kenting Street is where visitors are offered with a complete selection of foods while embraced by a relaxing holiday ambience. In addition, fresh tropical fruits and seafood can also be found along the street.

Alishan Scenic Mountain Area

Alishan Scenic Mountain Area Alishan’s mountain railway is an ever popular tourist destination with both local and foreign tourists. The train winds its way to the top of the mountain where there are plenty of easy hiking trails that offer fantastic photo opportunities. Alishan Scenic Mountain Area, Zhongzheng Village, Alishan township, Chiayi county, Taiwan
